"""
============================================================
HOW TO WRITE DEFAULT_TEXT FOR NATURAL SPEECH
============================================================

The structure of DEFAULT_TEXT strongly affects the quality
of speech generated by the Qwen3-TTS model.

Even when the instruction prompt stays the same, the way
sentences are written can change:

• pacing
• pause placement
• speech rhythm
• naturalness


GENERAL RULES
-------------

1. Write sentences the way people speak in real conversation.

2. Avoid long paragraph-style sentences.

3. Prefer short sentences with one idea each.

4. Use commas to create small pauses.

5. Use periods to clearly separate ideas.

6. For better pacing, use line breaks between major thoughts.

7. Avoid complex or academic sentence structures.


GOOD STRUCTURE EXAMPLE
----------------------

DEFAULT_TEXT = (
    "There is one simple idea in Atomic Habits that changes how we think about progress.\n"
    "Most people believe success comes from big, dramatic changes.\n"
    "But the author suggests something much simpler.\n"
    "Real improvement usually comes from small actions repeated every day.\n"
    "A one percent improvement might seem insignificant today,\n"
    "but over time those small gains begin to compound.\n"
    "And eventually, the results become surprisingly powerful."
)


WHY THIS STRUCTURE WORKS
------------------------

The line breaks create natural pauses in speech.

Each sentence expresses one clear idea, which helps the
TTS model maintain a conversational rhythm.


WHAT TO AVOID
-------------

Avoid large paragraph blocks like this:

    "Success is often misunderstood because many people
    believe dramatic change is required for improvement
    when in reality progress usually comes from consistent
    small actions repeated over time."

Long sentences like this often cause:

• rushed speech
• unnatural pacing
• robotic delivery


BEST PRACTICE WORKFLOW
----------------------

When generating speech:

1. Convert the source text into conversational sentences.

2. Break large paragraphs into short spoken lines.

3. Use line breaks between ideas.

4. Then pass the text to the TTS model.

This improves naturalness significantly without changing
the model or the instruction prompt.
